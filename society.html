Health Tips for Your Community

Monsoon Season Health Precautions

  • Avoid walking through stagnant water to prevent waterborne diseases
  • Avoid walking through stagnant water to prevent waterborne diseases
  • Keep your surroundings clean and free from garbage to prevent mosquito breeding
  • Drink only filtered or boiled water during the rainy season
  • Wash fruits and vegetables thoroughly before consumption
  • Keep ORS (Oral Rehydration Solution) packets handy for diarrhea emergencies

Diabetes Prevention in Rural Communities

  • Include more millets and whole grains in your daily diet
  • Replace sugary beverages with water or buttermilk
  • Take a 30-minute walk every day, preferably in the morning
  • Limit consumption of processed foods and fried snacks
  • Get your blood sugar checked at least once every six months

Maternal & Child Health Essentials

  • Pregnant women should attend at least four antenatal check-ups
  • Exclusively breastfeed your baby for the first six months
  • Ensure complete immunization of your child as per schedule
  • Monitor your child's growth using the health card provided
  • Include iron-rich foods in the diet of pregnant women and children

Rural Health Connect Program

Our Rural Health Connect program aims to bridge the healthcare gap between urban and rural areas through:

  • Mobile Health Units: Fully equipped medical vans that visit remote villages on a scheduled basis
  • Telemedicine Centers: Village-level facilities where residents can consult with specialists via video call
  • Health Volunteers: Local community members trained in basic healthcare, first aid, and health monitoring
  • Emergency Transport Network: Volunteer drivers with vehicles available to transport patients during emergencies
  • Health Education: Regular workshops and training sessions on preventive healthcare and early disease detection

Currently operational in 150+ villages across the region, this initiative has already improved healthcare access for over 200,000 people in rural areas.
